Diuretic Therapy Decision Support

Stepped assessment of congestion and diuretic titration — based on the 2019 HFA-ESC Position Statement (Mullens et al., Eur J Heart Fail 2019;21:137–155)

Step 1 — Initial Loop Diuretic Dose

Confirm congestion is due to volume overload before initiating. Have patient empty bladder and start urine collection immediately after first dose.

Patient Profile

Pre-Dose Checklist

1. Empty bladder (consider bladder scan) before first dose.
2. Start urine collection immediately after first dose.
3. Begin parallel monitoring: HR, rhythm, RR, SpO₂, signs of hypoperfusion, baseline weight, BP.
4. Consider early: continue GDMT, early MRA if not contraindicated, salt/water restriction, IV K⁺/Mg²⁺ if low.

Step 2 — Early Evaluation (First 6 Hours)

At 2 h: check spot urinary sodium. At 6 h: assess average hourly urine output over the period.

Combination Diuretic Therapy (Sequential Nephron Blockade)

Maximal loop diuretic dose reached with insufficient output. Add agents in sequence:
First line — Thiazide / thiazide-like diuretic
Metolazone 2.5–10 mg PO (give several hours before loop dose — slow absorption, peak up to 8 h)
or Chlorothiazide 500–1000 mg IV (onset ~30 min — give closer to loop dose)
or HCTZ 25–100 mg PO
Second line — Acetazolamide or amiloride
Acetazolamide 500 mg PO/IV — targets proximal nephron sodium avidity, reduces RAAS activation via macula densa effects.
Amiloride 5–10 mg PO.
Third line — SGLT2 inhibitor
Additional natriuretic agent targeting the proximal tubule (e.g. empagliflozin/dapagliflozin) — consider if not already on GDMT-dose SGLT2i.
Bail-out

If congestion persists despite maximal combination therapy → consider ultrafiltration. CARRESS-HF showed stepped pharmacologic care achieves equal decongestion to UF with fewer serious adverse events — UF is not first-line.

Chronic Ambulatory Dosing Principles

• Loop diuretics: Class I across full LVEF spectrum to prevent congestion — limited mortality/morbidity evidence in chronic HF.

• Use lowest effective dose; re-assess need after CRT or sacubitril/valsartan initiation.

• Avoid chronic ambulatory thiazide (sequential nephron blockade) where possible — risk of severe, undetected electrolyte disturbance outpatient.

• Salt intake: avoid >6 g NaCl/day (2.4 g Na⁺); avoid excessive fluid intake. Routine severe restriction (<2 g/day) not recommended.

• If furosemide pre-admission, consider switching to bumetanide or torsemide for more predictable absorption.
Source: Mullens W, Damman K, Harjola VP, et al. The use of diuretics in heart failure with congestion — a position statement from the HFA-ESC. Eur J Heart Fail 2019;21:137–155.
